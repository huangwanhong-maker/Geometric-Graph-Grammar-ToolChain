# Plan: higher-dimensional geometric grammars (2-simplices as generative primitives)

> **Status:** plan / research direction. NOT implemented. The toolchain implements the paper's
> graph (1-complex) algorithm first; this document sets the target for going to higher dimension.
> **Builds on:** [2-complex-induction.md](2-complex-induction.md) (the induction/reduction conjecture).
> **Origin:** Wanhong HUANG, 2026-06-27.

## 1. The core consideration

Take the **2-simplex (triangle)** as the *generative primitive* of the grammar — not the vertex or
the edge. A geometric graph grammar rewrites a node into a subgraph; the higher-dimensional grammar
rewrites a **cell into a sub-complex**, and the atom out of which structure is built is the 2-simplex.

Two-simplices are the right atom because:

- They are the lowest-dimensional simplex with intrinsic *shape* (an edge has only length; a triangle
  has angles/area — genuine 2D geometry, which is what our scale-invariant isomorphism compares).
- They tile surfaces, and surfaces are the boundaries of volumes, so a triangle grammar reaches
  3D and beyond through gluing (Section 2).
- Much of discrete geometry **and** discrete physics is already written in triangles (Section 4).

## 2. The dimensional ladder: glue k-simplices to make (k+1)

A `(k+1)`-simplex is bounded by `k+2` many `k`-simplices (its faces), and conversely a `(k+1)`-simplex
is obtained by **coning** a `k`-simplex to a new apex vertex. So simplices climb dimension by gluing:

```
points (0)  --glue-->  edges (1)  --glue-->  triangles (2)  --glue-->  tetrahedra (3)  --> ...
```

- **Gluing rule.** Two `k`-simplices sharing a `(k-1)`-face combine into a larger `k`-complex; a fan
  of `k`-simplices around a common `(k-1)`-face, capped by an apex, closes up into a `(k+1)`-simplex.
- **Recursion.** Because every dimension is reached from the one below by the same gluing operation,
  a grammar whose primitive and whose rewrite both act on simplices is, in principle, **dimension-
  agnostic** — one engine parameterized by `d`.

> *To firm up:* the exact combinatorial statement ("`k+2` `k`-simplices bound one `(k+1)`-simplex";
> conditions under which a set of glued `k`-simplices is the boundary of a single `(k+1)`-simplex vs.
> a more general `k`-manifold). State it precisely before relying on it.

### The reduction-to-2D conjecture (from the companion note)

Conjecture: induction in dimension `m` reduces to repeated **2-complex** problems, because the
`2`-skeleton (vertices+edges+triangles) plus the gluing data already determines most of the
combinatorial/geometric content, and higher cells are reconstructed by the same recursive gluing.
**Open:** whether the 2-skeleton + gluing is *sufficient* (lossless) or only *approximate*; what
gluing metadata must be retained across the reduction. This is the central theoretical question.

## 3. What the grammar becomes

| | paper (1-complex) | this plan (k-complex) |
|---|---|---|
| object | geometric graph `G=(V,E)` | geometric **simplicial complex** (k-simplices + incidence) |
| primitive | vertex / edge | **2-simplex** (and, by recursion, k-simplex) |
| isomorphism | graph-iso + similarity `T` | **complex-iso** (incidence-preserving) + similarity `T` |
| induction step | vertex expansion (add a vertex) | **simplex expansion** (glue a simplex along a face) |
| production rule | `q → S / B` (node → subgraph + embedding) | `σ → K / B` (cell → sub-complex + embedding) |
| scoring | frequency × convex-hull **area** | frequency × **k-volume** (k-content) |
| rewrite | erase node, splice subgraph, reconnect | erase cell, splice sub-complex, **re-glue faces** |

The rewriting rules are *local simplicial rewrites*. There is a canonical, complete such family — the
**Pachner (bistellar) moves** — which relate any two triangulations of the same PL-manifold by local
flips. A geometric grammar's production rules can be read as **geometry-decorated, learned Pachner
moves**: the grammar discovers *which* local rewrites recur in a given geometry and encodes them.

## 4. The physics bridge (why 2-complexes matter beyond graphics)

Several frameworks in discrete/quantum geometry are **literally** built from simplices — often
2-simplices — so a generative+inductive grammar over them is more than an analogy:

- **Spin foams (Loop Quantum Gravity).** A spin foam *is* a labeled **2-complex** (faces, edges,
  vertices), the history ("worldsheet") of a spin network; faces carry spins, edges carry
  intertwiners. The 2-complex is the fundamental object — exactly our generative primitive.
- **Pachner moves ↔ production rules.** The dynamics/equivalence of triangulations is generated by
  local bistellar moves; our learned production rules are the data-driven, geometric counterpart.
- **Causal Dynamical Triangulations (CDT) & simplicial quantum gravity.** Spacetime is assembled by
  gluing `d`-simplices (triangles in 2D, 4-simplices in 4D); the path integral sums over such gluings.
- **Regge calculus.** Piecewise-flat GR on a simplicial manifold; in 4D, curvature (deficit angle)
  is concentrated on the **2-simplices** (triangular hinges). Triangles are where geometry "lives".
- **Tensor models / group field theory.** Rank-`d` tensors generate random simplicial complexes by
  gluing `(d-1)`-simplices along faces — a stochastic grammar over simplices.

Potential payoffs of the bridge (speculative, to be tested):
- *Inverse problem:* given a triangulated geometry (a spin-foam 2-complex, a CDT configuration),
  **induce** a compact generative grammar — a learned, geometry-aware move set.
- *Forward:* **generate** candidate triangulations/foams from a grammar (a structured, non-uniform
  prior over complexes), as an alternative to uniform Pachner-move Monte Carlo.
- *Coarse-graining:* the grammar's hierarchical encoding (replace a sub-complex by one cell) is a
  natural, geometry-respecting **renormalization / coarse-graining** step on simplicial geometry.

## 5. Roadmap (incremental, each phase usable on its own)

1. **k-complex data model.** A generic `SimplicialComplex` (simplices by dimension + incidence/face
   maps + vertex coordinates), with the graph (1-complex) as the `d=1` special case so the existing
   2D engine is recovered exactly. Land in `src/g3-3d/` first for `d ≤ 3`.
2. **Geometric complex isomorphism.** Incidence-preserving iso + best similarity alignment + a
   quantized canonical key (the higher-D analog of `isomorphism.canonical_form`), with `k`-volume in
   place of convex-hull area. Reuse the determinism/tolerance design.
3. **Simplex expansion + detection.** Grow complexes by gluing a simplex along a shared face; score
   by frequency × k-volume; merge isomorphic results; the same order-by-order loop.
4. **Encoder + rewrite.** Production rules `σ → K / B`; rewriting that erases a cell and re-glues a
   sub-complex (face-matched), i.e. learned Pachner-like moves; hierarchical encoding.
5. **2-complex track (the conjecture).** Specialize/validate the "reduce m-D to 2D" claim on
   triangulated surfaces and 3-complexes; measure where the 2-skeleton+gluing is lossless.
6. **Physics interface (exploratory).** Import/export simplicial complexes in a spin-foam / CDT
   friendly format; try inducing a grammar from a known triangulation and regenerating it.

## 6. Open questions

- Precise gluing/reduction theorems (Section 2) and the conditions for losslessness.
- Canonicalization and isomorphism for simplicial complexes under similarity in `d ≥ 3` (cost,
  determinism, tolerance) — the analog of the paper's polar-grid quantization in higher dimension.
- Orientation/causality: oriented simplices and (for CDT) a causal/foliated structure — extra data
  the grammar must carry and the isomorphism must respect.
- Relation to the paper's 2D edge-based induction: are 2-simplex and edge induction equivalent on
  planar inputs, or do they capture different pattern sets? (Likely different — see companion note.)
- Whether "learned Pachner moves" are a useful, well-defined object: completeness, invertibility,
  and whether grammar generation gives a sensible measure on triangulations.
